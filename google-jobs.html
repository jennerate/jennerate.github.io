Context

Google recently created Jobs Board, a job aggregation tool that scrapes job postings found on various websites. Google Jobs aims to reduce the repetitive job postings that users see when visiting multiple job boards, and does a pretty good job (ha-ha) at that. When I was using the tool, I noticed a number of usability related issues that made the content both congested and, at points, overwhelming. As a designer, this made me wonder.

Process

Usability Testing

I knew I needed to get out of my head as much as possible to validate or reject my initial assumptions. I conducted a usability test based on Jakob Nielson's 10 user interface heuristics with 5 participants and tasked participants with the following scenario.

You are applying to your dream job on the Google Jobs Board

I asked participants to provide a stream of consciousness as they acted out the prompt. In an attempt to mimic real life I did not provide hints unless asked, as participants are usually unable to ask the person next to them for help. This was strategic because it identified usability issues that would otherwise go unnoticed by receiving prompts.
